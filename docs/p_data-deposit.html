Depositing data

Depositing your data provides a permanent, durable home for your data once you have completed your active research.

Key Notes

When depositing or storing data, you should be familiar with the following topics…

  • Personally identifiable or other sensitive information. Learn more about de-identifying data.
  • Content from Indigenous communities. Read more about Indigenous data governance.
  • Rights, commitments or obligations to share or deposit the data. Read more about sharing data.
  • Total size of your data. Not all repositories accept large datasets. See limitations on a couple of UBC supported repositories.
  • Costs associated with depositing your data. Read on to learn more.

As always, if you are unsure about any of the above, or have questions, get in touch.

UBC Repositories

> UBC Dataverse

UBC Dataverse is a free research data repository for our faculty, students and staff. It is a repository that supports research data from many disciplines. Researchers are encouraged to make their data available publicly, but can choose to restrict access to their data if they wish.

Key features of UBC Dataverse include:

  • UBC CWL integration.
  • No data deposit fees.
  • Support from the Research Data Management Librarian.
  • Dedicated Dataverses for yourself or your department / lab / research group are available.
  • Canadian data storage platform.
  • 2.5 GB maximum individual file size.
  • For datasets larger than a few GB, please contact us before proceeding.
  • Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs) at the dataset level.
  • Different licensing models (CC0, CC BY 4 and others) are available.
  • Standardized metadata and a nationwide data discovery platform.
  • Datasets are incorporated automatically into FRDR, making them findable alongside other Canadian research data.

For more information about Dataverse, consult our Dataverse page.

> FRDR

Canada’s Federated Research Data Repository allows faculty members, librarians or their designates at any Canadian post secondary institution to deposit research data.

Key features of FRDR include:

  • No data deposit fees.
  • Data curation review and support by the FRDR data curation team.
  • Up to 1 TB of storage space for each researcher by default; more is available on request.
  • Supports individual file sizes larger than 2.5 GB.
  • Canadian data storage platform.
  • Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs) at the dataset level.
  • Data deposits via HTTP or Globus file transfer (for large datasets).
  • Dedicated collections for yourself or your department / lab / research group are available.
  • Standardized metadata and a nationwide data discovery platform.
  • Geospatial data search with Geodisy.

For more information about FRDR, consult our FRDR page.

> Dryad

Dryad is a non-profit U.S. organization that operates a global, generalist research data repository. Founded in 2008 to serve researchers in the biological and medical sciences, Dryad has since grown to become a major repository for research data in many fields. UBC became the first Canadian institutional member of Dryad in 2021.

Key features of Dryad include:

  • UBC CWL integration.
  • Data curation review and support by the Dryad data curation team.
  • No fees for basic data deposits because UBC is an institutional member. Deposit fees may apply for very large deposits.
  • Data quality and accessibility verification, metadata cleanup and curation and direct support to researchers.
  • Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs) at the dataset level.
  • Mandatory public domain dedication CC0 license for all datasets.
  • Datasets are incorporated automatically into the UBC Dryad2Dataverse collection on the UBC Dataverse and into FRDR.

For more information about FRDR, consult our Dryad page. Link coming soon.

Non-UBC Options

There are numerous data repositories to support specific disciplines and generalist repositories to support a mix of disciplines. Sometimes specialist repositories are more appropriate than the UBC supported options.

While we can offer guidance on selecting a repository, these repositories are administered by bodies other than UBC and you need to contact those bodies directly for support.

Some common examples include:

  • GenBank for genetic sequence data
  • Global Biodiversity Information Facility for biodiversity data
  • Figshare for many different disciplines
  • Zenodo for many different disciplines (hosted at CERN)

Find more repositories

re3data.org is registry of research data repositories that will help you identify either disciplinary or institutional repository to share research data.

Your subject librarian can advise on other available repositories in your field.
