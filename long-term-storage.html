Long term storage

Definition

A place to deposit non-active research outputs such as data and scripts. These may be tied to a publication or stand alone products.

Considerations

Questions to ask yourself before proceeding:

  • Does my data contain personally identifiable information? If yes, we encourage you to send us an email before proceeding. Prompt - UBCO or UBCV?
  • Do I own the right to deposit this data? If the answer is no or you don’t know, we encourage you to send us an email before proceeding. Prompt - UBCO or UBCV?
  • Does my data include participant data from Indigenous communities? f yes, we encourage you to send us an email before proceeding. Prompt - UBCO or UBCV?

UBC Hosted / Supported Options

Dataverse

Canadian hosted, UBC supported long term data archiving. Perfect for data files that do not exceed 4 Gigabytes. Don’t know how big that is? This is probably perfect for you.

There are two options available for deposit:

  1. a generic UBC research area;

  2. your own self administered portal.

Which option is right for you? Do you need to deposit data to support a publication? Pick option A. Do you run a lab or research group and need to manage and coordinate multiple data sets? Pick option B.

Get Started

Option A is self guided. Get walk through guidance on your first deposit here. Link to Library

Option B is a guided process to get you set up. Email us and we’ll get you sorted. Prompt - UBCO or UBCV?

Non-UBC Hosted / Supported Options

There are many discipline and generic repositories in which you can deposit your data. Sometimes these are more appropriate to use than the generic options UBC has to offer. Sometimes the best source of suggestions will come from other experts in the field. While we can offer guidance on selection, these repositories are administered by bodies other than UBC and those bodies should be contacted directly for support.

Some common examples include:

  • GenBank for genetic sequence data
  • GBIF for biodiversity related data
